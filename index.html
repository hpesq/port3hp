What does a Medical Examiner do? 

- A Medical Examiner is the person who is an appointed physician under the law that determines the cause, time, and reason of death.

- When a death is in question by the law, this pertains to the manner that the death has appeared to happen. This may mean that the body was found deceased in ways that appear violent, without thurough explination, and injuries not caused by self harm.

- An autopsy is the examination of the body of the deceased who has been approved to be examined. The body is systematically examined on the outside and inside to assess the cause of death.
